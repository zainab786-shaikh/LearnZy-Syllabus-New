4.4 Fire Hazards; Fire Drill

What are Fire Hazards?

A fire hazard is anything that can start a fire, make it spread, or cause harm due to fire. Fire hazards are dangerous because they can lead to property damage, injuries, or even loss of life.

Types of Fire Hazards

Flammable Materials

Objects that can easily catch fire, like paper, wood, gasoline, or alcohol.

Example: A pile of newspapers near a stove can catch fire if exposed to heat.

Electrical Hazards

Faulty wiring, overloaded circuits, or damaged electrical appliances can cause fires.

Example: A short circuit in a faulty extension cord can cause sparks and start a fire.

Improper Handling of Fire

Leaving stoves, candles, or fireplaces unattended.

Example: A burning candle left near a curtain can cause a house fire.

Gas Leaks

Leaks from LPG cylinders or gas pipelines can cause explosions.

Example: A gas leak in the kitchen can ignite when a match is lit.

Blocked Fire Exits

Fire exit doors or staircases blocked by furniture, boxes, or trash.

Example: In case of a fire, if a door is blocked, people cannot escape safely.

Improper Storage of Chemicals

Storing chemicals like paint, fuel, or cleaning solutions near heat sources.

Example: A bottle of paint thinner kept near a heater can catch fire easily.

What is a Fire Drill?

A fire drill is a practice session where people learn how to react safely in case of a fire emergency. It helps in preparing individuals to evacuate quickly and efficiently.

  • Improve Response Time â€“ Faster evacuations save lives.
  • Identify Problems â€“ Helps in finding obstacles like locked exits or faulty alarms.

Steps in a Fire Drill

Sound the Alarm

A fire alarm or bell is rung to signal an emergency.

Stop What You Are Doing

People should leave their work, avoid using elevators, and prepare to evacuate.

Follow the Escape Plan

Move towards designated exits in an orderly manner.

Example: In an office, employees should follow the marked exit signs.

Use Fire Extinguishers (If Trained)

Small fires can be put out using fire extinguishers.

Example: If a trash can is on fire, a trained person can use an extinguisher to stop it.

Assemble at a Safe Location

A pre-decided meeting point should be used.

Example: In a school, students gather in the playground after evacuation.

Headcount and Reporting

Managers or teachers check if everyone is present.

Example: A teacher ensures all students have left the classroom safely.

Return After Clearance

Once officials confirm there is no danger, people can return.

Real-Life Examples of Fire Drills

1. Fire Drill in Schools

Schools conduct fire drills to train students and staff.

Example: A school rings the alarm, students evacuate in a line, gather in the ground, and teachers check attendance.

2. Fire Drill in Offices

Companies conduct fire drills for employee safety.

Example: Employees practice exiting through staircases and gathering at a safe spot.

3. Fire Drill in Hospitals

Hospitals practice drills to protect patients and staff.

Example: Nurses move non-mobile patients using stretchers to a safe location.

4. Fire Drill in Apartments

Residential buildings organize drills to teach residents how to escape.

Example: Tenants learn how to exit using stairwells instead of elevators.

Safety Tips for Fire Prevention

  • Never overload electrical sockets.
  • Do not leave stoves or candles unattended.
  • Store flammable materials safely.
  • Conduct regular fire drills.
  • Keep fire extinguishers in accessible places.
