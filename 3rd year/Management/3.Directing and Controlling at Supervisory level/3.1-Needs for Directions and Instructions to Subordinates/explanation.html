3.1 Needs for Directions and Instructions to Subordinates

Why Do Employees Need Directions and Instructions?

Employees need clear directions and instructions to work efficiently. If instructions are unclear, employees may get confused, leading to mistakes, delays, or poor performance.

Important Aspects of Giving Directions

Clarity

The supervisor must communicate clearly so that employees understand what needs to be done.

Completeness

Instructions should cover all necessary details to avoid confusion.

Feasibility

Instructions should be practical and realistic to implement.

Example

Imagine a factory where workers assemble mobile phones. The supervisor must:

  • Clearly explain how to assemble each part.
  • Ensure workers know the safety rules.
  • Give step-by-step guidance to new workers.

If instructions are incomplete, workers may assemble parts incorrectly, leading to defective products.
