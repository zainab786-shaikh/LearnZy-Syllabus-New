3.4 Managerial Control

Understanding Teams and the Link Between Various Departments

Every company has multiple departments, such as production, sales, finance, human resources, and customer service. For an organization to function smoothly, all these departments must work together.

Example:

  • A mobile phone manufacturing company has:
    • A design team that creates the phone model.
    • A production team that assembles the phone.
    • A quality control team that checks if the phone meets standards.
    • A sales team that markets and sells the phone.
    • A customer service team that handles complaints.

If the quality control team finds defects in the phone, they must inform the production team to fix them before selling. This link between departments ensures quality products and customer satisfaction.

Process and Quality Standards

Every company sets standards to maintain quality and efficiency. These standards define how products or services should be delivered.

Example:

  • A fast-food chain like McDonald's has strict standards:
    • A burger must be made in 5 minutes.
    • The temperature of the patty must be at least 70Â°C.
    • Customer waiting time should not exceed 3 minutes.

If employees fail to meet these standards, the supervisor must take corrective action.

Steps in the Control Process

The control process has four main steps:

1. Setting Performance Standards

A standard is a benchmark that employees must follow. It can be related to quality, quantity, cost, or time.

Example:

  • A car manufacturing company may set a standard that:
    • A worker must assemble 10 car engines per day.
    • The rejection rate (defective products) should be less than 2%.

2. Measuring Performance

The next step is to track actual performance and see if employees are meeting the set standards.

Example:

  • A supervisor in a call center checks how many calls each agent attends in a day and whether they resolve customer issues.

3. Comparing Performance with Standards

After measuring performance, managers compare the results with the set standards.

Example:

  • If the target is 10 car engines per day and a worker makes only 7, there is a shortfall.
  • If a call center expects agents to solve 90% of customer complaints but they solve only 75%, performance is low.

4. Correcting Unfavorable Deviations

If actual performance is below the standard, corrective action is needed.

Example:

  • If a worker assembles only 7 car engines instead of 10, the supervisor may provide training or add more workers.
  • If a call center agent is not solving complaints properly, they may receive extra coaching.
