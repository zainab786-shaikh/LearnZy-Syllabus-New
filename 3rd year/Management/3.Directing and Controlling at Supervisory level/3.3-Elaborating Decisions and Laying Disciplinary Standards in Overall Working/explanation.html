3.3 Elaborating Decisions and Laying Disciplinary Standards in Overall Working

Making Decisions Clear

A supervisor must explain the reasons behind their decisions so that employees understand their importance and follow them willingly.

Laying Disciplinary Standards

Every workplace has rules that employees must follow. A supervisor ensures that:

  • Employees come to work on time.
  • Work is done as per company guidelines.
  • There is no misconduct like stealing, dishonesty, or poor behavior.

Example:

  • In a call center, if an employee speaks rudely to a customer, the supervisor must explain the company’s customer service rules and take disciplinary action if needed.
  • In a hospital, nurses must follow strict hygiene rules. If someone doesn’t, the supervisor must correct them immediately to ensure patient safety.

Conclusion

Directing at the supervisory level is about guiding employees properly, predicting and preventing mistakes, and ensuring discipline in the workplace.

A good supervisor communicates clearly, supports employees, and enforces company rules effectively.
