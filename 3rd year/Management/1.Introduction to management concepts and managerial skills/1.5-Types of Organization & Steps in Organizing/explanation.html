1.5 Types of Organization & Steps in Organizing

A. Types of Organization

An organization is a group of people working together to achieve a common goal. Different organizations follow different structures based on their size, goals, and work style.

1. Line Organization

Definition: In this type of organization, authority flows from the top level to the bottom level in a direct chain of command. Each employee reports to only one boss.

Example:

  • In the army, the General commands the Colonel, the Colonel commands the Major, and so on.
  • In a small shop, the owner gives orders to the manager, the manager gives orders to workers.

Advantages:

  • Simple to understand
  • Clear roles and responsibilities
  • Quick decision-making

Disadvantages:

  • Heavy workload on top managers
  • Not flexible for large businesses

2. Functional Organization

Definition: In this structure, the organization is divided into different departments based on functions like Marketing, Finance, HR, Production, etc. Each department has its own head.

Example:

  • A company may have a Sales Department, an HR Department, and a Finance Department, each managed by specialists.
  • A hospital has different functional units: doctors, nurses, finance, reception, and housekeeping.

Advantages:

  • Specialization in each department
  • Efficient management
  • Work is divided properly

Disadvantages:

  • Coordination between departments can be difficult
  • Employees may be confused about who to report to

3. Line and Staff Organization

Definition: This is a combination of line organization and functional organization. Here, line managers handle decision-making and staff experts provide advice.

Example:

  • In a manufacturing company, the production manager takes decisions (line authority), while technical experts suggest ways to improve efficiency (staff authority).
  • In a hospital, the chief surgeon (line authority) takes decisions, while medical researchers (staff authority) give advice on new treatments.

Advantages:

  • Expertise is used effectively
  • Better decision-making
  • Workload is shared

Disadvantages:

  • Can be expensive due to hiring of experts
  • Conflict between line managers and staff experts

4. Project Organization

Definition: A temporary organizational structure created for a specific project. Once the project is completed, the team is dissolved.

Example:

  • A construction company forms a project team to build a bridge. After the bridge is completed, the team is dissolved.
  • A movie production team works together on a film, then moves to new projects after the movie is done.

Advantages:

  • Flexible and adaptable
  • Teamwork and innovation
  • Focused on specific goals

Disadvantages:

  • Uncertain job security for employees
  • Coordination may be difficult

5. Matrix Organization

Definition: A combination of functional and project-based structures. Employees report to both a functional manager and a project manager.

Example:

  • In a software company, a programmer reports to both the Technical Head (functional manager) and a Project Leader (project manager).
  • In a car manufacturing company, an engineer reports to both the Design Head and the Production Head.

Advantages:

  • Efficient resource use
  • More flexible
  • Better coordination across departments

Disadvantages:

  • Employees may get confused about reporting to multiple managers
  • Can lead to conflicts

B. Steps in Organizing

Step 1: Identifying Objectives

First, the organization must define what it wants to achieve.

Step 2: Identifying and Grouping Activities

The next step is to list all the necessary activities and group similar tasks together.

Step 3: Assigning Responsibilities

The organization assigns tasks to different individuals or departments.

Step 4: Establishing Authority and Responsibility

The organization defines who will report to whom (hierarchy).

Step 5: Coordinating Efforts

The organization ensures that different departments work together smoothly.

Step 6: Reviewing and Improving the Organization

The organization must check its performance regularly and make improvements if needed.

Conclusion

Different organizations follow different structures based on their needs. Organizing is an important management function that helps businesses plan, assign tasks, coordinate efforts, and achieve goals efficiently.
