1.2 Management Characteristics and Principles, Levels of Management, and Their Functions

1. What is Management?

Management is the process of planning, organizing, leading, and controlling resources (people, money, materials, etc.) to achieve specific goals effectively and efficiently.

Imagine you are organizing a birthday party. You need to:

  • Decide the date, venue, and guest list (Planning)
  • Arrange decorations, food, and music (Organizing)
  • Guide and instruct people to do their tasks (Leading)
  • Ensure everything is happening as planned (Controlling)

This is how management works in businesses, schools, hospitals, and every organization.

2. Characteristics of Management

  • Goal-Oriented – The main purpose of management is to achieve a goal.
  • Continuous Process – Management is ongoing and never stops.
  • Universal – Management is required in all types of organizations.
  • Multidisciplinary – It includes different fields like finance, marketing, and human resources.
  • Dynamic – Management must adapt to changes in the business environment.
  • Group Activity – It involves teamwork and cooperation.

3. Principles of Management

Management principles are guidelines that help managers run an organization efficiently. Henri Fayol introduced 14 principles of management:

  • Division of Work – Work should be divided among employees to increase efficiency.
  • Authority and Responsibility – A manager should have the right to give orders but must also take responsibility.
  • Discipline – Employees should follow company rules and respect authority.
  • Unity of Command – An employee should receive orders from only one boss.
  • Unity of Direction – A team should work towards a common goal under one plan.
  • Remuneration – Employees should be paid fairly for their work.

4. Levels of Management and Their Functions

1. Top-Level Management (Strategic Level)

These are the highest-ranking managers responsible for major decisions.

  • Who are they? CEOs, Managing Directors, Board of Directors.
  • Functions: Make long-term decisions, set company goals, represent the company to outsiders.

2. Middle-Level Management (Tactical Level)

These managers act as a link between top and lower levels.

  • Who are they? Department Managers, Branch Managers.
  • Functions: Implement policies, supervise lower-level managers, ensure team coordination.

3. Lower-Level Management (Operational Level)

They directly handle workers and daily activities.

  • Who are they? Supervisors, Team Leaders, Foremen.
  • Functions: Assign tasks, ensure work quality, solve workers’ problems.

5. Management vs. Administration vs. Organization

Feature Management Administration Organization
Definition Execution of plans Setting policies A structured setup
Main Role Implementing goals Deciding goals Coordinating activities

6. Relationship Between Management and Administration

Management and administration work together to ensure smooth operations.

Example:

  • A hospital’s administration decides to introduce a 24-hour emergency service.
  • The management hires doctors, arranges medical supplies, and sets up shifts.
  • The organization provides doctors, nurses, and infrastructure to run the service.

Conclusion

Management ensures an organization runs efficiently by planning, organizing, leading, and controlling.

It has three levels: top, middle, and lower, each with specific roles.

Principles like division of work, unity of command, and discipline help in better management.

Management, administration, and organization are interconnected, ensuring an organization functions smoothly.
