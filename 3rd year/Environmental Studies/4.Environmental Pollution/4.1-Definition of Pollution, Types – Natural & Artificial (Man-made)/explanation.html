What is Pollution?

Pollution is the introduction of harmful substances into the environment, making it unsafe or unhealthy for living beings. These harmful substances, called pollutants, can come from nature or human activities. Pollution affects the air, water, and land, causing damage to plants, animals, and humans.

For example:

  • If a factory releases smoke into the air, it makes the air dirty and difficult to breathe.
  • If waste is thrown into a river, the water becomes polluted and unsafe to drink.

Types of Pollution

Pollution can be classified into two main types based on its source:

  • Natural Pollution
  • Artificial (Man-made) Pollution

1. Natural Pollution

Natural pollution occurs due to natural events that introduce harmful substances into the environment without human involvement.

Examples of Natural Pollution:

  • Volcanic Eruptions ‚Äì Volcanoes release ash, smoke, and harmful gases like sulfur dioxide into the air.
    Example: The eruption of Mount St. Helens in 1980.
  • Forest Fires ‚Äì Wildfires produce smoke, carbon dioxide, and ash.
    Example: Amazon rainforest wildfires.
  • Dust Storms ‚Äì Strong winds carry large amounts of dust, reducing visibility.
    Example: The Dust Bowl in the 1930s.
  • Pollen from Plants ‚Äì Some plants release pollen, causing allergies.
    Example: Seasonal pollen allergies in spring.
  • Landslides & Soil Erosion ‚Äì Heavy rains wash away soil, reducing water quality.
    Example: Himalayan landslides.

Key Fact: Natural pollution is not caused by humans, but it can still harm the environment and people.

2. Artificial (Man-made) Pollution

Artificial pollution is caused by human activities like industries, vehicles, and waste disposal.

Examples of Artificial Pollution:

  • Air Pollution from Factories & Vehicles ‚Äì Smoke from factories and car exhausts releases harmful gases.
    Example: Air pollution in Delhi.
  • Water Pollution from Sewage & Chemicals ‚Äì Dumping waste into rivers pollutes water.
    Example: The Ganga River pollution.
  • Land Pollution from Garbage & Deforestation ‚Äì Throwing plastic and cutting down forests.
    Example: Landfills filled with plastic waste.
  • Noise Pollution from Traffic & Machines ‚Äì Loudspeakers, honking cars, and construction activities.
    Example: Noise pollution in Mumbai.
  • Light Pollution from Cities ‚Äì Excessive artificial lights disturb sleep cycles.
    Example: Bright city lights affecting birds.

Key Fact: Artificial pollution is caused by humans and can be controlled by adopting eco-friendly habits.
