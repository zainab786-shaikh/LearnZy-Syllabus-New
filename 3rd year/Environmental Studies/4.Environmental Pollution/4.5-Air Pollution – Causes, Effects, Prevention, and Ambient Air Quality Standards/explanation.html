4.5 Air Pollution – Causes, Effects, Prevention, and Ambient Air Quality Standards

What is Air Pollution?

Air pollution occurs when harmful substances such as gases, dust, smoke, or chemicals are released into the air, making it unsafe for humans, animals, and plants. It reduces air quality and can lead to serious health and environmental problems.

Causes of Air Pollution

1. Natural Sources of Air Pollution

  • Volcanic Eruptions – Releases ash, sulfur dioxide, and other gases.
  • Forest Fires – Generates smoke and carbon monoxide.
  • Dust Storms – Dust particles affect air quality.
  • Pollen from Plants – Can cause allergies and contribute to air pollution.

2. Man-Made Sources of Air Pollution

  • Burning of Fossil Fuels – Releases CO₂, SO₂, and NOx.
  • Industrial Emissions – Factories emit sulfur dioxide, carbon monoxide, and dust.
  • Deforestation – Reduces oxygen and increases carbon dioxide levels.
  • Agricultural Activities – Pesticides and fertilizers release airborne toxins.
  • Household Activities – Burning wood or charcoal pollutes indoor air.

Effects of Air Pollution

1. Health Effects

  • Respiratory Problems – Asthma, lung infections, and chronic bronchitis.
  • Heart Diseases – Increases risk of heart attacks and high blood pressure.
  • Eye and Skin Irritation – Smog and chemicals cause irritation.
  • Cancer – Long-term exposure to toxic pollutants may cause lung cancer.

2. Environmental Effects

  • Acid Rain – SO₂ and NOx mix with rainwater, damaging crops and water bodies.
  • Global Warming – Greenhouse gases trap heat, causing climate change.
  • Ozone Layer Depletion – CFCs destroy the ozone layer, increasing UV exposure.
  • Harm to Wildlife – Polluted air affects habitats and species.

3. Damage to Buildings and Monuments

Acidic gases cause blackening and deterioration of structures.

Prevention of Air Pollution

1. Individual Efforts

  • Use Public Transport or Carpooling.
  • Use Bicycles or Walk.
  • Plant More Trees.
  • Reduce, Reuse, Recycle.
  • Use Energy-Efficient Appliances.

2. Industrial and Government Efforts

  • Use of Renewable Energy.
  • Installing Pollution Control Devices.
  • Strict Laws and Regulations.
  • Encouraging Electric Vehicles (EVs).
  • Reducing Deforestation.

Ambient Air Quality Standards (AAQS)

Defines safe limits of pollutants in the air.

Pollutant Sources Effects Safe Limit (India’s AAQS)
Particulate Matter (PM2.5, PM10) Vehicle exhaust, industries Lung diseases, heart problems PM2.5 – 40 µg/m³ (Annual)
Sulfur Dioxide (SO₂) Burning coal, power plants Acid rain, respiratory issues 50 µg/m³ (Annual)
Nitrogen Oxides (NOx) Vehicles, industries Smog, lung irritation 40 µg/m³ (Annual)
Carbon Monoxide (CO) Incomplete fuel burning Reduces oxygen in blood, dizziness 2 mg/m³ (8-hour)
Ozone (O₃) Vehicle emissions, sunlight reaction Breathing issues, plant damage 100 µg/m³ (8-hour)

Conclusion

Air pollution is a serious problem affecting health, the environment, and economies worldwide. It is caused by both natural and human activities, but most pollution is preventable with the right measures. Governments, industries, and individuals must work together to control air pollution and ensure a cleaner, healthier future.
