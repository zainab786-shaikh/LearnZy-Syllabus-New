1.2 Segments of Environment

The environment is everything around us, including air, water, land, and living beings. It is made up of four main segments:

  • Atmosphere (Air)
  • Hydrosphere (Water)
  • Lithosphere (Land)
  • Biosphere (Life)

1. Atmosphere ‚Äì The Air Around Us

The atmosphere is the layer of air that surrounds the Earth. It contains gases like oxygen (which we breathe), carbon dioxide (used by plants), and nitrogen.

Examples of Atmosphere:

  • When we breathe, we take in oxygen from the atmosphere.
  • Clouds and rain form in the atmosphere.
  • Aeroplanes fly in the atmosphere.
  • Birds and insects move through the atmosphere.
  • The wind that we feel on our face is part of the atmosphere.

The atmosphere also protects us from harmful rays of the sun and keeps the Earth warm.

2. Hydrosphere ‚Äì The Water on Earth

The hydrosphere includes all the water bodies on Earth, such as oceans, rivers, lakes, and even underground water.

Examples of Hydrosphere:

  • The ocean is the largest part of the hydrosphere.
  • Rivers like the Ganga, Nile, and Amazon flow through different countries.
  • Lakes and ponds are small water bodies where fish and plants live.
  • Rainfall is also part of the hydrosphere.
  • The ice caps of Antarctica and glaciers are frozen water, but they still belong to the hydrosphere.
  • Underground water (borewell or well water) that people use for drinking also comes from the hydrosphere.

Without water, life cannot exist. That‚Äôs why the hydrosphere is very important.

3. Lithosphere ‚Äì The Land We Live On

The lithosphere is the solid part of the Earth, including mountains, valleys, soil, rocks, and even the land where we build houses.

Examples of Lithosphere:

  • Mountains like the Himalayas and Alps are part of the lithosphere.
  • Deserts like the Sahara are dry parts of the lithosphere.
  • Forests, farmlands, and cities are all located on the lithosphere.
  • Volcanoes come from the lithosphere when molten rock (lava) comes out.
  • Sand, soil, and rocks are all part of the lithosphere.

The lithosphere provides us with soil for farming, minerals for industries, and land for living.

4. Biosphere ‚Äì The World of Living Beings

The biosphere includes all living things ‚Äì humans, animals, plants, and even tiny bacteria. It is where life exists, and it depends on the atmosphere, hydrosphere, and lithosphere for survival.

Examples of Biosphere:

  • Humans, animals, and plants all belong to the biosphere.
  • Forests are full of life ‚Äì birds, insects, trees, and animals.
  • Oceans have fish, whales, corals, and tiny microbes.
  • Bacteria in the soil help plants grow.
  • Bees and butterflies help in pollination.
  • Even in extreme places like deep oceans and high mountains, life exists in the biosphere.

How These Segments Are Connected

All four segments of the environment are connected. Here‚Äôs how:

  • A tree (biosphere) grows in the soil (lithosphere), takes in carbon dioxide from the air (atmosphere), and absorbs water from the rain (hydrosphere).
  • Fish (biosphere) live in lakes and oceans (hydrosphere), which are affected by rainfall (atmosphere) and surrounded by land (lithosphere).
  • Humans (biosphere) need oxygen (atmosphere), water (hydrosphere), and land (lithosphere) to survive.

Conclusion

The four segments of the environment ‚Äì Atmosphere, Hydrosphere, Lithosphere, and Biosphere ‚Äì work together to support life on Earth.

  • The Atmosphere provides air.
  • The Hydrosphere provides water.
  • The Lithosphere provides land and resources.
  • The Biosphere is where life exists.
