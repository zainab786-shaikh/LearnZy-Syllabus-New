5.3 Management Measures

Management measures refer to the steps taken to protect and manage natural resources like water, land, and energy in a sustainable way. These measures help in conserving the environment while ensuring that resources are available for future generations.

1. Rainwater Harvesting (RWH)

What is it?

Rainwater Harvesting is the process of collecting and storing rainwater for future use instead of letting it run off. This water can be used for drinking, irrigation, and recharging groundwater.

Methods of Rainwater Harvesting:

  • Rooftop Rainwater Harvesting – Rainwater from rooftops is collected in tanks or directed into the ground.
  • Surface Runoff Harvesting – Water from streets, gardens, and other surfaces is collected and stored.
  • Recharge Pits & Wells – Water is allowed to seep underground to refill groundwater.

Examples:

  • ✅ Chennai has made rainwater harvesting compulsory for buildings, leading to an increase in groundwater levels.
  • ✅ Rajasthan’s Johads (traditional ponds) store rainwater for drinking and irrigation in dry areas.
  • ✅ In Bangalore, apartments store rooftop rainwater and use it for gardening and washing.

Benefits:

  • ✔ Saves water for future use.
  • ✔ Reduces flooding.
  • ✔ Helps in areas with water shortages.

2. Groundwater Recharge

What is it?

Groundwater Recharge is the process of refilling underground water by allowing rainwater to seep into the ground. This helps prevent water depletion.

Methods of Groundwater Recharge:

  • Recharge Wells & Pits – Rainwater is diverted into deep pits to seep into the soil.
  • Percolation Tanks – Large tanks are used to hold water so it slowly seeps underground.
  • Check Dams – Small dams are built on rivers to slow down water flow and allow water to seep into the ground.

Examples:

  • ✅ In Gujarat, the Sardar Patel Participatory Water Conservation project has created check dams to recharge groundwater.
  • ✅ Punjab and Haryana farmers build recharge pits to improve underground water levels.
  • ✅ Delhi Metro uses recharge wells at stations to refill groundwater.

Benefits:

  • ✔ Increases water availability.
  • ✔ Reduces dependence on borewells.
  • ✔ Prevents land sinking due to overuse of groundwater.

3. Green Belt Development

What is it?

Green Belt Development means planting trees and vegetation in and around cities, industries, and roads to improve the environment. It acts as a natural filter for air pollution and helps in reducing heat.

Examples:

  • ✅ Delhi’s Ridge Forest helps in controlling air pollution.
  • ✅ Mumbai's Aarey Forest is a green belt that absorbs carbon dioxide.
  • ✅ Chandigarh’s green cover makes the city cooler and cleaner.

Benefits:

  • ✔ Reduces pollution.
  • ✔ Provides shade and cools the area.
  • ✔ Prevents soil erosion and floods.

4. Use of Renewable Energy

What is it?

Renewable energy comes from natural sources that never run out, like the sun, wind, and water. Using renewable energy reduces pollution and saves fossil fuels.

Types of Renewable Energy:

  • Solar Energy – Using sunlight to generate electricity.
  • Wind Energy – Using wind turbines to produce power.
  • Hydro Energy – Generating electricity from flowing water.
  • Biogas – Using organic waste to produce energy.

Examples:

  • ✅ India’s Bhadla Solar Park (Rajasthan) is one of the world’s largest solar farms.
  • ✅ Tamil Nadu’s Muppandal Wind Farm generates wind energy for thousands of homes.
  • ✅ Tehri Dam (Uttarakhand) produces hydroelectricity for multiple states.

Benefits:

  • ✔ Reduces pollution.
  • ✔ Saves non-renewable resources like coal and oil.
  • ✔ Lowers electricity costs in the long run.

5. Watershed Management

What is it?

A watershed is an area where all water drains into a common point like a river or lake. Watershed management involves protecting and managing land and water resources in these areas.

Methods of Watershed Management:

  • Afforestation (Planting Trees) – Prevents soil erosion and improves water retention.
  • Building Small Check Dams – Slows down water flow and increases groundwater recharge.
  • Contour Farming – Farmers plow across slopes to stop water runoff.

Examples:

  • ✅ Sukhomajri Project (Haryana) restored forests and water bodies using watershed techniques.
  • ✅ Hiware Bazar (Maharashtra) became a model village by implementing watershed management.
  • ✅ Karnataka’s Watershed Projects have improved irrigation in dry areas.

Benefits:

  • ✔ Prevents floods and droughts.
  • ✔ Increases crop productivity.
  • ✔ Improves soil and water quality.
